    Methodology

    How we tested — in the open

    Purged & embargoed cross-validation

    Returns are split into folds with a purge gap and embargo between train and test, so information cannot leak across the boundary. We report the median fold and the worst fold — not a single lucky split.

    Deflated Sharpe (multiple testing)

    Test enough variants and one will look brilliant by luck. We raise the bar using a deflated Sharpe threshold that accounts for the number of trials, sample length, skew and kurtosis. Your Sharpe must clear that bar — not just beat zero.

    Transaction costs & slippage

    We re-run the strategy net of fees, funding and realistic slippage. The gap between gross and net Sharpe is often where a "great" edge quietly disappears.

    Tail risk & CVaR

    Averages hide the days that matter. We measure CVaR (expected loss in the worst tail) and a tail-adjusted Sharpe, so a smooth-looking curve with a brutal left tail can't pass as safe.

    Drawdown & survivorship

    We track maximum drawdown over the full path and flag survivorship and data-quality caveats — selecting only the periods or assets that survived would flatter any result.

    One honest pass — no retries

    The verdict reflects a single, locked out-of-sample run. We do not re-tune until it passes. A verdict is probabilistic, not a promise — but it is the same standard we hold our own strategies to.
